Vigil, explained from zero
1. What is this thing, in one breath?
There's a scam where criminals call someone, pretend to be police or CBI, say "there's a warrant for your arrest, pay us now or you go to jail," and scare people into sending money. It's called the digital arrest scam.
Vigil is a system you built to fight that scam. A regular person forwards the scary message (a voice recording, a photo of a fake warrant, or just the text) to Vigil. Vigil reads it, decides in a few seconds whether it's a scam, and warns the person. Then it quietly remembers the scammer's phone number, payment ID, and fake badge number — so when the next victim reports the same criminals, Vigil goes "I've seen these guys before, they've hit 14 people already."
That's it. Warn the victim, and connect the dots between victims to expose the whole gang.
2. The two halves of the app
Think of Vigil as a building with two entrances.
Entrance 1 — the public door (the "Sandbox"). This is for ordinary scared people. It looks like a WhatsApp chat. They paste the scam message, and Vigil replies with a verdict: 🚨 red "this is a scam, hang up," ⚠️ amber "be careful," or ℹ️ "looks fine." Simple. No login.
Entrance 2 — the staff door (the "Control Plane"). This is for investigators — the police/analysts. It's password-protected. Inside, they see every report coming in, plus tools to visualize the criminal networks. This is the fancy dashboard.
3. The pipeline — what happens in those few seconds
When a report comes in, it goes down an assembly line:
If there's a voice recording → it gets transcribed into text (a service called Groq Whisper does this — it's a free, fast "speech-to-text" robot).
If there's a photo (like a fake warrant) → the text in the image gets read out (this is the "vision" step).
All the text goes to an AI (Groq Llama 3 — a free chatbot brain) which is told: "Pull out the phone numbers, payment IDs, badge numbers, and rate how threatening this is from 0 to 100."
