Product Requirements Document (PRD): Vigil

Unified AI Intelligence Platform for Proactive Fraud Network Disruption

1. Executive Summary

The Real Problem:
In 2024, "digital arrest" scams defrauded Indian citizens of over Rs 1,776 crore. These are industrialized, cross-border operations. Yet, law enforcement treats them as isolated, individual cases. When a victim reports a fake "CBI Warrant" or a coercive video call, that data sits in static relational tables. The connective tissue between these thousands of isolated complaints is completely lost, allowing fraud compounds to operate with impunity.

Why Existing Solutions Fail:
Current systems are reactive data-entry sinks. They cannot ingest and process messy, multimodal evidence at the point of contact. More importantly, they lack automated relationship synthesis. Cops do not need more lists of complaints; they need to see how they connect.

Why Vigil is Different (The Shift to Intelligence):
Vigil does not just log complaints—it synthesizes actionable intelligence. The graph is not our product; it is our proof. Vigil's breakthrough is its ability to take a single, panicked citizen report (a voice note or a forged warrant) and instantly run a composite AI extraction and graph-relation traversal.

In seconds, the platform proves that a new scam call is not an isolated event, but the 15th node of an active transnational syndicate, mapping its exact infrastructure for immediate law enforcement disruption.

2. Product Vision

Vision: To collapse the time between fraud initiation and network disruption from months to milliseconds.

Mission: Equip investigators with an autonomous, multi-modal intelligence pipeline that translates edge panic into structured, court-admissible syndicate maps.

Product Identity: A premium, high-integrity control plane designed for regional cybercrime units—fast, clinical, and undeniable.

The "One-Week-Later" Judge Takeaway:
"That platform that turned a single panicked scam call into a fully mapped criminal network in real time using a completely free open-source stack."

1. Nodal Cyber Investigator (The Hunter - Primary User)

Goals: Target the orchestrators and "money mule" coordinators of fraud rings rather than playing whack-a-mole with low-level callers.

Frustrations: Drowning in thousands of disconnected Excel sheets and FIRs (First Information Reports) that actually share the same digital fingerprints.

Responsibilities: Lead inter-jurisdictional cyber raids, freeze mule accounts, and coordinate regional response.

Workflow: Stares at the Vigil Command Center. When a new report comes in, they watch it immediately snap into a larger, glowing cluster. They click the cluster to generate a ready-to-use raid profile.

2. The Vulnerable Citizen (The Sensor - Edge Reporter)

Goals: Instant verification of whether the "ED/CBI Officer" threatening them on a Skype call is real or a scammer.

Frustrations: Cyber helplines are busy; they are under extreme psychological coercion and terrified of being arrested.

Responsibilities: Provide raw, unstructured evidence (screengrabs, voices, payment links) to the platform.

Workflow: Forwards the scammer's voice note or a PDF warrant to the Vigil WhatsApp channel and gets a clear, calm safety verdict within 5 seconds.

4. Product Scope

IN SCOPE (The Core Loop)

Vigil Signal (Dual Ingestion): A production-ready WhatsApp Webhook pipeline + a visually identical Web Sandbox UI (to guarantee demo delivery regardless of Wi-Fi or API timeouts).

Vigil Nexus (The Intelligence Engine): A Neo4j-powered relational traversal engine that maps extracted entities (Phones, UPI IDs, Badges, IP Addresses) in real time.

Vigil Atlas (Spatial Layer): A geospatial visualization layer tracking real-time hot spots, showing the localized distribution of mule accounts and scam origins.

Vigil Casebook (Actionable Reports): An automated generator that packages clustered graph data into an audit-ready, legal-admissible PDF intelligence brief.

OUT OF SCOPE (Hackathon Traps - Rejected Features)

Real-time telephony network interception: Highly complex, legally gated, and impossible to demo genuinely.

Multi-role permission hierarchies: Standard authentication is sufficient; complex RBAC wastes precious coding hours.

Counterfeit physical currency scanning: A massive distraction from the "digital arrest" narrative. We are strictly tracking digital networks.

The Intake (Signal): A citizen under psychological coercion forwards a 20-second WhatsApp voice note of a scammer's threats along with an image of a forged "Supreme Court Arrest Memo."

The Parallel Pipeline:

The audio is transcribed via Whisper (extracting core threats).

The document image is parsed by a local instance of Florence-2 (extracting badge numbers, fake signatures, and the target bank account/UPI ID).

The Assessment: Signal instantly sends a tiered, calm notification to the citizen: "🚨 CRITICAL ALERT: Verified Scam Signature. Communication matches 14 known extortion cases. Cease contact immediately."

The Nexus Detonation: Behind the scenes, these newly extracted entities are injected into Neo4j. The engine runs a graph similarity query.

The Reveal (Command Center): On the investigator's UI, a notification flashes. On the map (Atlas), a new dot appears. On the graph (Nexus), the dot animates and connects itself directly to a massive, existing 45-node cyber-fraud ring. The investigator sees that the fake badge used on this victim is the exact same one used in 12 other cases across 3 states.

Disruption (Casebook): The investigator clicks "Export Casebook." The system instantly generates a court-ready intelligence pack containing the cluster data, cross-jurisdictional matches, and bank account linkages.

6. AI Architecture: The Zero-Budget Local Stack

To guarantee absolute reliability, speed, and zero cost, Vigil uses a hybrid local/free-tier pipeline.

                  │ Audio/Image   │
                  └──────┬────────┘
                         │
         ┌───────────────┴───────────────┐
         ▼                               ▼
  [Audio: Whisper]              [Image: Florence-2]
(Text Transcript)             (Local OCR/Doc Parsing)
         │                               │
         └───────────────┬───────────────┘
                         ▼
               [Groq API: Llama 3]
         (Entity Extraction & JSON Structuring)
                         │
                         ▼
             [Graph Reasoning Agent]
          (LangChain -> Neo4j Cypher)


1. The Ear (Whisper - STT)

Deployment: Run via Groq's free-tier whisper-large-v3 endpoint OR run locally via a lightweight Python package (openai-whisper).

Task: Converts raw conversational audio from WhatsApp into structured, plain-text transcripts.

2. The Eye (Florence-2 - VLM)

Deployment: Run locally on the Python/FastAPI backend using Hugging Face Transformers (microsoft/Florence-2-base or large).

Why Florence-2? It is exceptionally lightweight (~232M parameters), executes in milliseconds locally, and excels at document layout analysis, optical character recognition (OCR), and visual grounding. This eliminates external vision API costs and rate-limit panics during the demo.

Task: Reads the forged document. Identifies and extracts text from seals, signatures, counterfeit government badges, and printed banking details.

3. The Brain (Groq API - Llama 3 70B)

Deployment: Hosted via Groq's high-speed, free-tier API.

Task: Takes the raw transcript from Whisper and the structured OCR tokens from Florence-2. It performs context-aware entity matching, detects extortive script templates, and outputs a strict JSON payload.

4. The Relationship Engine (LangChain + Neo4j Cypher)

Deployment: Backend service.

Task: Receives the Groq JSON output. It dynamically structures a MERGE Cypher query to write the nodes into Neo4j AuraDB without duplicating existing records (e.g., if the phone number already exists, it updates the "SCAM_COUNT" and draws a new relationship).

9. Feature Specification

Feature 1: Signal (Edge Triage & Intake)

Purpose: Secure, friction-free citizen reporting with low-panic response design.

Inputs: Raw WhatsApp media payload (voice recording + document capture) or Web UI inputs.

Backend Logic (Confidence-Tiered Alerts):
To satisfy the judging criteria of a near-zero false-positive rate, Signal avoids binary "scam/not-scam" claims.

Threat Score > 85% (Matches active template or flagged entity):
Verdict: CRITICAL -> "🚨 IMMEDIATE THREAT: This matches an ongoing extortion operation. Hang up immediately. Vigil has logged this profile."

Threat Score 45-84% (Suspicious script elements detected, but no database entity match):
Verdict: CAUTION -> "⚠️ EXERCISE CAUTION: This communication exhibits strong high-pressure tactics. Avoid sharing financial details. Please verify independently."

Threat Score < 45% (Irrelevant or normal communication):
Verdict: CLEAR -> "ℹ️ NOTICE: No patterns of extortion detected. If you remain concerned, visit an official police portal."

Success Criteria: Under 6 seconds round-trip processing time from raw upload to user verdict.

Feature 2: Nexus (Relationship Engine)

Purpose: Turn isolated, unstructured variables into a clear relationship profile.

User Story: As an investigator, I want to see the immediate network context of a newly reported scam caller so that I can see who is supplying their bank accounts.

Graph Schema:

Nodes: Citizen, Phone, UPID, BadgeNumber, Bank_Branch, Syndicate_Cluster.

Edges: REPORTED, USED_IN, LINKED_TO, MULE_FOR.

Success Criteria: Instant render of 2nd-degree connections of any newly added node.

Feature 3: Atlas (Geospatial Threat Map)

Purpose: Identify physical operation hubs, localized money-mule clusters, and coordinates of active fraud operations.

Backend Logic: Geocodes cell towers, IP ranges, or bank branch locations extracted from evidence. Clusters them geographically using standard Leaflet marker clustering.

Transparency Requirement: All geospatial points generated during the mock run must display a subtle visual tag in the UI: [Illustrative Demo Data] to protect presentation integrity.

Feature 4: Casebook (Court-Admissible Export)

Purpose: Generate a dense, legally sound intelligence package from an investigation in one click.

Inputs: Selected Syndicate_Cluster node.

Outputs: A beautifully formatted, structured PDF document containing:

Chronological record of linked complaints.

Specific evidence hashes (MD5 signatures of original audio and document images stored in Supabase).

The visual Nexus map layout proving cross-jurisdictional account sharing.

A drafted formal block for presentation to cyber-crime judicial magistrates.

12. Hackathon Strategy & Differentiation

Why Vigil Wins (Judges' Perspective)

Fewer Moving Parts, High Execution Stability: While other teams struggle with multi-tier API orchestrations, slow external vision APIs, or custom LLM hosting, Vigil uses a robust local model (Florence-2) that guarantees instant document analysis with zero latency or external dependency failure.

Clear Technical Depth: Combining graph theory (Neo4j) with localized multimodal parsing (Whisper + VLM) is an advanced architectural pattern that commands respect.

Cohesive, Purpose-Driven Workflow: We are presenting a single story. Every feature we show serves the exact purpose of taking data from a victim's handset and putting an actionable file onto an investigator's desk.

Anticipated Judge Questions & Technical Defenses

Q: Why use a graph database over PostgreSQL for relationship mapping?

Defense: Relational tables require highly expensive recursive JOIN operations to map relationships past 2 degrees of separation. Neo4j handles multi-degree traversals (e.g., finding paths between different victims, banks, and phones) instantly using index-free adjacency.

Q: Is Florence-2 performant on standard servers?

Defense: Yes. Florence-2 is highly optimized (base model is under ~250M parameters). It runs efficiently on standard CPUs and is lightning fast on basic GPUs, making it ideal for cost-efficient local deployment.
